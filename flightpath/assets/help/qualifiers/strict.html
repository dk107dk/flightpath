Qualifier: strict

strict can be applied to decimals and integers to force them to be interpreted in a way that matches their purpose, visually as well as practically.

Specifically, strict:

  • Requires that decimals have a decimal point, meaning a '.' character, in keeping with having a fractional value, despite that fractional value being zero.
  • Disallows whole number integers that have a decimal point, despite having no fractional value, in keeping with functionally being a whole number.
